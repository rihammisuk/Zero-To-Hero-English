Part 01 · Foundation

Mindset — train your brain before your tongue

You did not learn to ride a bicycle by reading about wheels. You sat on the seat, fell, got up, and tried again. Speaking is the same. Mistakes are the pedals — without them, the bicycle does not move.

Why your brain goes blank

It goes blank for one reason: you try to do three jobs at once — ① think the idea in Bengali, ② translate it to English, ③ check the grammar. Three jobs at once = a traffic jam in the brain = blank.

The fix

Reduce three jobs to one. Speak in small, ready-made pieces you have already practised. Say "I want to…" a hundred times in practice and your mouth says it with no translation, no grammar check, no traffic jam. This whole course loads your brain with ready-made pieces.

How to stop translating from Bengali

StepMethodExample
1Learn in chunks, not single words. A chunk is a group of words that always travel together.Learn "have breakfast" — not "have" + "breakfast" apart.
2Connect words to pictures, not to Bengali words."apple" → imagine a red apple, not the word আপেল.
3Name things around you in English all day.While walking: "bus… shop… tree… cold…"
4Self-talk for 2 minutes a day — mistakes allowed."I am making tea. The water is hot. I need sugar."
5Accept simple sentences. A simple correct one beats a perfect Bengali one stuck in your head.Think "Food is good" instead of freezing.

How to think in English (it grows like a plant)

Week 1–2 single words: tea, bag, friend, rain Week 3–4 tiny sentences: "I am tired." Month 2 plans: "I will go to work, then study." Month 3+ opinions: "I think this is boring."
🕐
The English Hour

Choose one hour every day — your bus ride, your cooking time. In that hour, every thought must be in English, even baby English. One hour of English thinking daily beats five hours of grammar reading.

The Rule of 3 — kill repetition

Rule of 3

Every time you learn a new word, immediately make 3 spoken sentences with it. A word used 3 times by your mouth moves from "I understand it" to "I can say it." This is how we stop you repeating the same five words forever.

✅ Part 1 Practice
  1. Write: 5 things near you right now in English. Then 3 tiny sentences: "I am ___." / "I like ___." / "I want ___."
  2. Speak (2 min): Describe what you are doing now. Baby English allowed — "I am sitting. I am reading. The room is quiet."
  3. Bengali → English: আমি ক্লান্ত → I am tired. · এটা ভালো → This is good. · আমি চা চাই → I want tea.
  4. Self-test: What are the 3 jobs that make your brain blank? What is the Rule of 3? What is the English Hour?
Part 02 · Foundation

The 80/20 Rule — learn less, speak more

A menu may have 100 dishes, but 80% of customers order the same 20. English has 170,000+ words, but daily talk uses a small set. We learn that small set first, deeply.

What covers 80% of daily English

You needHow muchWhere
Core words~1000 most common wordsPart 3
Grammar for speakingOnly 11 tools (not 500 rules)Part 4
Sentence patterns~50 reusable structuresPart 5
Conversations16 real-life situationsPart 6
Pronunciation~12 problem soundsPart 10

What to ignore for now

✕ Rare grammar (past perfect continuous) ✕ Dictionary words (magnanimous, ubiquitous) ✕ Writing words (furthermore, henceforth) ✕ Memorizing grammar rule names
The 80/20 promise

Master only Parts 3, 4 and 5 and you can already survive in shops, buses, university, phone calls and small talk. Everything after that is polish.

✅ Part 2 Practice
  1. Write the 5 places where YOU need English most — university, work, shop, phone, doctor. This is your 80%. Keep it.
  2. Say out loud: "I don't need all of English. I need MY English. I will learn the most useful words first."
  3. Bengali → English: আমার ইংরেজি দরকার দোকানে, কাজে, এবং ফোনে → I need English in the shop, at work, and on the phone.

Part 04 · Walls

Speaking Grammar — only the grammar your mouth needs

Grammar is the GPS of a car. You don't need to know how the satellite works — you need it to take you to the right place. Here are 11 tools, in order. Each uses the words from Part 3. The order matters: 4.1 is the foundation of everything.

Tool 1 — am / is / are (the "to be" engine)

In Bengali we say "আমি ক্লান্ত" with no verb. English ALWAYS needs a verb. When there is no action, English uses am / is / are as the glue.

PersonGlueExample
IamI am tired. I am from Bangladesh.
He / She / It · one thingisShe is a doctor. The tea is hot.
You / We / They · manyareWe are friends. The shops are closed.
Memory trick

I-AM, one-IS, many-ARE. Say it like a rhythm: "I am, he is, they are" ten times fast. Your mouth must know it, not your memory.

Tool 2 — have / has (owning & experiencing)

have is your pocket — things, family, problems, time. "আমার আছে" = I have.

PersonVerbExample
I / You / We / TheyhaveI have a class today. They have two children.
He / She / IthasShe has a new job. He has a fever.
have breakfasthave a showerhave a lookhave timehave a questionhave a headache

Tool 3 — Present Simple (daily habits & facts)

Your daily-routine camera — things you do every day, always. The only rule: with he/she/it, the verb takes -s.

I work · you work · we work — he works · she studies · it rains
Use forExamples
HabitsI drink tea every morning. She goes to work by train.
FactsThe sun rises in the east.
TimetablesThe class starts at 9. The bus leaves at 5:30.
Frequency words go before the verbI usually cook at night. I never smoke.

Tool 4 — Present Continuous (happening NOW)

The live camera — what is happening this minute. Formula: am/is/are + verb-ing. (Tool 1 lives inside Tool 4 — this is why order matters.)

Now (live)Every day (routine)
I am cooking dinner.I cook dinner every night.
It is raining. Look outside!It rains a lot in July.
They are waiting for the bus.They wait at this stop every day.
Bonus use

Near-future plans: "I am meeting my friend tomorrow." "We are moving next month."

Tool 5 — Past Simple (finished stories)

Your photo album — finished moments: yesterday, last week, in 2020. Regular verbs add -ed; irregular verbs change shape (go → went). Same for everyone — I went, she went, they went.

was / were = the past of am/is/are: I/he/she/it was · you/we/they were
The most powerful habit in this book

The "Yesterday Story" — every night before sleep, say: "Yesterday I woke up at… I went to… I met… I ate…" One minute, every night.

Tool 6 — Future (will & going to)

FormWhenExamples
going to + verbPlan made before speakingI am going to visit my family in December.
will + verbInstant decision, promise, predictionThe phone is ringing — I will answer it. It will rain tonight.
Memory trick

Plan → going to. Promise → will. Both are correct in most daily talk, so never freeze choosing — just speak.

Tool 7 — Present Perfect (past with a link to now)

Imagine mud on your shoes. The walking happened in the past, but the mud is visible NOW. Formula: have/has + V3 (third form).

UseExamples
Life experience (ever/never)Have you ever eaten sushi? I have never seen snow.
Just finished (just/already/yet)I have just finished. I haven't eaten yet.
Started before, still true (for/since)I have lived in Sydney for two years.
go→gonesee→seeneat→eatendo→donetake→takenwrite→writtenbe→been

Tool 8 — Negatives & Questions

Questions and negatives need a helper (do/does/did), like a rickshaw needs a puller. Exception: am/is/are, will, can are their OWN helpers.

TenseNegativeExample
am/is/are+ notI am not ready. It isn't cold.
Present simpledon't / doesn't + verbShe doesn't like coffee (NOT doesn't likes).
Past simpledidn't + verbI didn't go (NOT didn't went).
Futurewon't + verbI won't be late, I promise.
The WH machine

WH + helper + subject + verb? — What do you want? · Where does she live? · When did you arrive? · How do I get to the station? · How much is this?

Tool 9 — Modal Verbs (can, could, should, must, may, would)

Modals are spices — they add ability, advice, permission, politeness. Formula is always modal + base verb (never -s, -ed, or to).

ModalFlavourExample
can / can'tability, permissionI can drive. Can I sit here?
couldpolite request, past abilityCould you help me, please?
shouldadviceYou should see a doctor.
must / have tonecessityI have to work tonight.
may / mightpossibilityIt may rain. I might be late.
wouldpoliteness, imaginationI would like a coffee.
Politeness ladder

Give me water → Can you give me water? → Could you give me some water, please? In Australia, "Could you… please?" is your safest daily tool.

IN-ON-AT pyramid (time)

Big box → in (months, years: in June). Middle → on (days: on Friday). Small point → at (clock: at 5 pm). Big-in, day-on, time-at.

✅ Part 4 Grand Revision — the seven shapes

Take one idea — "study English" — and say it in every shape. When this flows without reading, Part 4 is yours:

  1. Habit: I study English every day.
  2. Now: I am studying English now.
  3. Past: I studied English yesterday.
  4. Future: I will study English tomorrow.
  5. Perfect: I have studied English for two months.
  6. Negative: I don't study at noon.
  7. Question: Where do you study English?
Part 05 · Walls

Sentence Patterns — your sentence machines

A pattern is a mould for making sweets (মিষ্টির ছাঁচ). The mould stays the same; you change the filling. "I want to ___" is one mould — pour in any verb and a perfect sentence comes out. Master ~50 moulds and you make thousands of sentences with zero translation.

Group A — Talking about me

PatternExamples
I am …I am a student. I am from Bangladesh. I am interested in technology.
I have …I have a question. I have two part-time jobs.
I feel …I feel tired today. I feel that this plan is risky.
I am good at … / bad at …I am good at cooking. I am bad at remembering names.

Group B — Wants, needs & plans

PatternExamples
I want to …I want to improve my English. I want to take a break.
I need to …I need to finish this today. I need to talk to you.
I would like to … (polite)I would like to order, please. I'd like to book a room.
I have to … (no choice)I have to work tonight. I have to submit it by Friday.
I am going to / planning to …I am going to call my family. I am planning to visit Dhaka.

Group C — Opinions & thoughts (the "smart speaker" group)

PatternExamples
I think … / I believe …I think the price is too high. I believe this plan will work.
In my opinion, …In my opinion, this is the best option.
The reason is … / That's why …The bus was full — that's why I walked.
I agree / I don't agree, because …I don't agree, because the cost is too high.
It depends on …It depends on the weather.

Group D — Past & experience

I used to … (past habit, now stopped) I have … before / I have never … When I was …, I … It was the first time I …

Group E — Asking & requesting (survival group)

Can I … ? / Could I … ? Could you … please? Do you know … ? Is it okay if I … ? How do I … ? What does … mean?

Group F — If, suggestions & soft language

If I …, I will … If I were you, I would … Why don't we … ? / Let's … Maybe we should … I'm not sure, but … Sorry, I didn't catch that.
Pattern stacking

Real fluency joins 2–3 patterns with connectors: "I think we should leave now, because if we wait, we will miss the train." Practise stacking two patterns every day.

✅ Part 5 Practice — Machine-gun drill
  1. Pick one pattern. Fire 10 sentences fast without stopping: "I want to eat. I want to sleep. I want to learn driving…" Speed kills translation.
  2. Bengali → English: আমার মনে হয় এটা ভালো বুদ্ধি → I think it is a good idea. · যদি সময় পাই, আমি তোমাকে ফোন করব → If I have time, I will call you.

Part 07 · Roof

Fluency Training — from sentences to speech

You now have ingredients (Part 3), methods (Part 4), recipes (Part 5), and full meals (Part 6). Fluency is cooking under time pressure — training your mouth to move WITHOUT the Bengali translator in your head.

⏱️
The 3-second rule

Start speaking within 3 seconds. Wait longer and your brain calls the Bengali translator. A simple sentence NOW beats a perfect sentence in 10 seconds.

The Story Skeleton (works for any story)

Skeleton

One day, ___ (when/where) → I was ___ (situation) → Suddenly, ___ (problem) → So I ___ (action) → Finally, ___ (result) → I felt ___ / I learned ___.

"One day last winter, I was waiting for the bus after my night shift. Suddenly, it started raining heavily, and I had no umbrella. So I ran to a shop and waited. The shopkeeper was kind — he gave me tea. Finally the rain stopped and I got home at 2 am. I felt tired but thankful. I learned: always keep an umbrella in my bag!"

The 1-3-5 minute talk ladder (weekly test)

LevelTaskPass when
1 minuteA simple topic: my family / my phone / my favourite foodYou speak the full minute with pauses, but never STOP.
3 minutesA story with the skeleton + your opinion at the endYou use first/then/because/finally, and finish with "I think…"
5 minutesMini-presentation: opening, 3 points, a closingClear structure: opening → 3 points → "to sum up".
Fillers Well… · Let me think… · How can I say it… · What I mean is… · Anyway…
👂
Speaking starts in the ears

15 minutes of listening a day feeds your speaking. Listen slightly below your level (~80% understood). One 3-minute clip watched 3 times teaches more than a 1-hour movie once. Use English subtitles, never Bengali — Bengali subtitles train translation.

The tennis rule — keep a conversation alive

Always hit the ball back

Answer + ONE extra detail + return question. Weak: "Where are you from?" → "Bangladesh." (dies). Strong: "Bangladesh — a city called Dhaka. It's very lively, a bit like Sydney traffic! How about you, are you from here?"

Common Mistakes of Bangladeshi Learners

Every cricketer from one region shares batting habits — they learned on the same pitch. Bengali speakers share the same English mistakes because Bengali grammar is our "home pitch." Knowing the difference removes them fast.

✕ Mistake✓ CorrectWhy
I am understand. / I am agree.I understand. / I agree.These are full verbs — no am/is/are.
He go to office. / She have a car.He goes to the office. / She has a car.English adds -s for he/she/it.
I didn't went. / She doesn't likes it.I didn't go. / She doesn't like it.The helper carries the tense — main verb stays base.
I am here since two years.I have been here for two years.Present perfect + for/since.
He is my cousin brother.He is my cousin."Cousin brother" is South-Asian English only.
I have a doubt.I have a question."Doubt" means suspicion, not question.
I am going to home.I am going home."Home" usually takes no "to".
She is more better. / most easiestShe is better. / easiestNever double the comparison.
I am having a car. / I am knowing him.I have a car. / I know him.State verbs (have, know, like, want) take no -ing.
Myself Riham.I am Riham. / My name is Riham."Myself + name" is not standard.
He is angry on me. / married withHe is angry with me. / married toPreposition partners are fixed — learn as chunks.
open the light / give an examturn on the light / sit an exam (AusE)Direct translation of "লাইট খোলা / পরীক্ষা দেওয়া".
Please do the needful. / I will revert you.Please do this for me. / I will get back to you.Old subcontinent office English — sounds odd here.

Part 10 · Polish

Pronunciation — clear beats perfect

Pronunciation is like handwriting. It doesn't need to be beautiful — it needs to be readable. We fix only the sounds that make Bengali speakers hard to understand. Goal: clear, not foreign.

SoundHow to fixPractice
V vs WV: top teeth touch bottom lip, buzz. W: round lips like blowing a candle, teeth touch nothing.very · water · window · "We were very wet."
TH (think)Tongue tip BETWEEN your teeth, blow air softly. See your tongue in a mirror.think · three · thank · "Thank you for those three things."
TH (this)Same tongue position, but add voice (throat vibrates).this · that · they · mother · weather
Z vs JZ is a buzzing S — smile, teeth close, buzz: zzzz.zero · zoo · busy · music · easy
S at word startStart with a long ssss, then finish. Never add "i" before it.school · station · speak · student
Silent lettersMemorize the famous silents.Wednesday → "WENZ-day" · half → "haaf" · know → "no"
Word stress = the secret

English punches ONE syllable. Wrong stress confuses listeners more than a wrong sound: a-BOUT · ho-TEL · com-PU-ter · uni-VER-si-ty. And the sentence punches meaning words: "I'm GOING to the STATION to MEET my FRIEND." Say small words quickly and softly.

🎬
Shadowing — the #1 exercise

Play a slow English clip. Pause after each sentence and copy it EXACTLY — sound, speed, music. Five minutes a day in front of a mirror for TH and V/W.
